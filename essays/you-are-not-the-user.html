You are not the user.

May 2026 · ~3 min read · By Rohan Kumar

As a Product Manager, I often see a common trap: PMs and even entire teams start believing they are the user.

At first glance, it feels harmless — after all, empathy is key, right? But confusing yourself with your users can quietly derail your product decisions.

Here's why

Bias

You have way more context, assumptions, and internal knowledge than a normal user. This clouds your judgment.

Different needs

You might prioritize things users don't care about. What you find "intuitive" could actually be confusing to them.

Over-optimization

You might overthink "cool" features that don't actually solve a real user pain.

Missed opportunities

Real users often behave irrationally, emotionally, and messily — not like the "logical" PM you are.

But here's the nuance

Your instincts are still valuable. Being close to the user can be a superpower — if you stay humble.

  • Use your instincts only to form hypotheses.
  • Always validate them with real user feedback.
Ultimately, your job isn't to be the user. It's to be the bridge — between user needs, business goals, and the messy real world.

Stay curious. Stay grounded.
